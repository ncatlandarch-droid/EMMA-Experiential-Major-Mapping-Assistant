# EMMA — Experiential Major Mapping Assistant

## What is EMMA?

EMMA (Experiential Major Mapping Assistant) is a revolutionary student success platform built by Think! Design and Planning, LLC for North Carolina A&T State University. EMMA transforms the traditional academic advising experience by providing students with an interactive, AI-powered career mapping companion that connects coursework to real-world career outcomes.

## The Problem EMMA Solves

University students often complete their degree requirements without understanding how each course connects to their career trajectory. Traditional advising focuses on credit hours and prerequisites, but misses the experiential dimension — the skills, competencies, and professional development milestones that actually prepare students for the workforce.

Students frequently graduate without:
- Understanding their industry's salary landscape and job market outlook
- Connecting classroom learning to professional competency standards
- Building a portfolio of experiential achievements (internships, research, service, leadership)
- Knowing what certifications or licensure exams they should be preparing for

## How EMMA Works

### The Four-Phase Journey

EMMA maps every student's degree program across four experiential phases:

1. **Explore (Year 1)** — Foundation courses and self-discovery. Students explore their field, join organizations, and build foundational knowledge. EMMA introduces them to career pathways and helps them understand where their degree leads.

2. **Engage (Year 2)** — Deeper immersion. Students engage with professional communities, begin research opportunities, and develop technical skills. EMMA tracks their progress and suggests relevant opportunities.

3. **Develop (Year 3)** — Professional skill building. Students complete advanced coursework, professional internships, and begin exam preparation. EMMA provides career outlook data and connects students with industry contacts.

4. **Launch (Year 4)** — Career readiness. Students complete capstone projects, licensure preparation, and launch into their careers. EMMA celebrates their achievements and provides placement resources.

### The Competency Matrix

Beyond coursework, EMMA tracks four categories of experiential development:

- **Purpose** — Academic and research engagement (undergraduate research, faculty mentorship, scholarly activities)
- **Communities** — Campus involvement (student organizations, leadership roles, Greek life, alumni networking)
- **LocalGlobal** — Broadening perspectives (study abroad, global exchange, community engagement)
- **Identity** — Professional development (career services, resume building, professional attire, networking)

### AI Career Counselor

EMMA features an AI-powered career counselor avatar named Emma who provides:

- **Smart Coaching** — Contextual advice based on which courses you've completed and what's coming next
- **Live Career Data** — Real-time salary information, job growth projections, and employment trends from the Bureau of Labor Statistics
- **Opportunity Feed** — Direct links to internships, research positions, career fairs, and campus events from platforms like Handshake
- **Program-Specific Guidance** — Each program's Emma knows the specific accreditation standards, licensure requirements, and industry expectations

### Integration Architecture

EMMA is designed to integrate with existing university systems:

- **Canvas LMS** — Embedded directly in the learning management system students already use
- **Handshake** — Live internship and job listings from the university's career platform
- **Banner/DegreeWorks** — Student records and degree audit data
- **BLS/CareerOneStop** — Live federal employment and salary data
- **NotebookLM** — AI-powered research and knowledge management

## Who Uses EMMA?

### Students
- Track progress through their experiential journey
- Discover career pathways and salary expectations
- Connect with opportunities (internships, research, service)
- Prepare for licensure and certification exams

### Advisors
- Monitor student cohort progress across all four phases
- Identify at-risk students who are falling behind on experiential milestones
- Generate progress reports for accreditation documentation

### Administrators
- View department-wide analytics and completion rates
- Track accreditation compliance across programs
- Measure experiential learning outcomes at scale

## The White-Label Model

EMMA is designed as a white-label platform that adapts to any degree program at any university. Each program gets:

- Custom branding with college-specific colors and icons
- Program-specific milestone timelines based on actual curriculum
- Relevant competency matrices aligned to industry standards
- Career data specific to that program's professional field

Currently built for NC A&T's College of Agriculture and Environmental Sciences (17+ programs), EMMA's architecture supports deployment across all 9 colleges and over 100 degree programs.
