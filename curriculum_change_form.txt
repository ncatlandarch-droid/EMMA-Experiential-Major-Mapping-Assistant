April 14, 2025


Dear Dr. Arnab and Members of the Curriculum Review Committee,


I am writing to submit proposed revisions to the Bachelor of Science in Landscape Architecture (BSLA) curriculum at North Carolina A&T State University, with the goal of implementing the changes beginning in Fall 2026. These revisions are the result of a comprehensive self-study and feedback from the most recent Landscape Architectural Accreditation Board (LAAB) review. The updates are designed to strengthen compliance with current accreditation standards, clarify course sequencing, reinforce program learning outcomes, and better prepare students for the planning and design responsibilities expected in professional practice. Key highlights of the proposal include:


1. Revised course descriptions and student learning outcomes for all LDAR-prefix courses to clearly reference the planning and design process, where applicable.
2. Improved prerequisite structure to reflect a more intentional progression of knowledge and skills across design studios, graphics, theory, and technical coursework.
3. Adjustments to course titles, notes, and catalog language for clarity, consistency, and alignment with LAAB expectations.
4. Continued adherence to the 120-credit hour graduation requirement for the BSLA degree, with no increase in credit hours or resource impact.


I appreciate your consideration of these revisions and welcome any questions or feedback as the committee reviews the proposed changes.
